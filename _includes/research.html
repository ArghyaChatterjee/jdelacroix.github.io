Graduate Research

Despite the collective push towards greater autonomy in robots, most applications still require human intervention to guide autonomous robots in complicated tasks. Existing interfaces allow a human to intuitively interact with one or a few robots; however, as the number of robots involved in the task grows large, these interfaces become less effective or even unusable due to a lack of scalability in the interaction.

Therefore, my Ph.D. research is focused on understanding these human-swarm interactions and developing new ways to control large networks of robots collectively with just a small number of simple inputs, rather than insisting that humans guide these robots invididually.
